4 March 2026

Wandsworth Net Zero Partnership Roundtable

The University of Roehampton hosted sector leaders, innovators and students from across London's public and private sectors to explore what it takes to build a greener, more resilient city together.

The roundtable brought together stakeholders from Wandsworth Council, A Greener London, South Thames College and industry practitioners alongside University of Roehampton students and Professor Ayse Demir. A student panel featuring Eva Idugboe, Gabriela Molina Murillas, Patrick Orji, Abena Abboah-Offei and Mohammed brought depth, honesty and lived experience into the conversation — shaping the tone of the entire session. The day reminded us that sustainability isn't just a policy ambition. It is a lived practice, built through partnership, shared intelligence and community-led ambition.

Data must be accessible to be transformative. Communities need clearer, more accessible environmental data. When residents, students and local organisations can see what is happening in their neighbourhoods, they are better equipped to make informed decisions and advocate for change.

Skills development is central to London's net zero transition. From green construction to digital sustainability tools, the demand for future-focused skills is growing rapidly. Universities can act as incubators for emerging talent, equipping students not just with knowledge but with agency, confidence and practical experience.

Collaboration is not optional — it is the engine of progress. No single sector can deliver net zero alone. The most impactful ideas emerged when policy, research, community insight and industry expertise intersected.

Sustainability must be lived, not just planned. The tour of SETEC and the Aubyn Square Passivhaus development demonstrated how learning environments can become catalysts for local transformation.

Honest conversations build stronger pathways. People naming challenges, acknowledging gaps and sharing lessons learned created space for more grounded, realistic and actionable ideas.

These insights are shaping how the Roehampton Sustainable Futures Hub continues its work — strengthening partnerships, deepening community engagement and building the skills and systems needed for a greener London.

6 May 2026

Chessington Industrial Estate Community Forum

Bringing together local businesses, workers, and community organisations to explore sustainability, green skills, and collaboration across the estate.

The first Chessington Industrial Estate Community Forum was hosted at King George Field Indoor Bowling Club, delivered in partnership with the University of Roehampton (Roehampton Sustainable Futures Hub), The Community Brain CIC, and local businesses across the estate.

Discussions covered sustainability and green skills, waste management and packaging challenges, business networking, and rebuilding the community connections that industrial estates lost following COVID-19. Attendees included representatives from National Rescue, The Protective Textile Company Ltd, Mollart Engineering, and many other businesses and workers from across the estate.

What stood out most was the willingness of participants to reconnect, contribute ideas, and engage in meaningful conversations around building a stronger and more resilient industrial community.

The project was also presented at the University of Roehampton's Academic Collaboration Showcase on 12 May 2026, where it attracted strong interest from academics, council representatives, SME professionals, engineers, healthcare workers, and sustainability organisations, including international visitors from the Netherlands. Several new partnerships and collaboration opportunities emerged from those conversations.

The next Community Forum is being planned. If you are a business, worker, or community member connected to Chessington Industrial Estate, we would love to hear from you.

12 May 2026

Roehampton Research & Innovation Showcase 2026

The University of Roehampton welcomed academics, industry partners and community organisations for an afternoon of collaboration, insight and impact — with the Roehampton Sustainable Futures Hub proud to be part of the day.

The showcase highlighted a wide range of research tackling real-world challenges, including gut microbiome and nutrition, heat regulation and human performance, AI chatbots in psychology, community partnerships across South London, dementia and puzzle-based therapy, homelessness and abuse research, and neurodiversity and gambling. Valued partners including BackToBalance, Penumbra and Age UK joined the day, helping extend the reach and impact of research beyond academia.

The Roehampton Sustainable Futures Hub contributed to the wider theme of community partnerships across South London. Led by Associate Professor Ayse Demir, the Hub showcased how research, student involvement and cross-sector collaboration can support practical sustainability action with communities, SMEs and local partners.

The Hub display highlighted three current projects — the Air & Living Infrastructure Initiative, Green Skills for South London, and the Kingston Hive Impact & Capacity Building Project — each demonstrating how evidence-led work responds to real local needs across air quality, green skills, community engagement and impact communication.

It was also a valuable opportunity to speak with academics, students, industry partners and community organisations who visited the Hub stand to learn more about our work and explore future collaboration.

Together, we are committed to driving innovation that supports communities, informs policy and creates meaningful social change. People. Planet. Partnership.
